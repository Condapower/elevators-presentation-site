Troubleshooting Example

Symptom: Car doesn’t run, handheld shows “Drive Fault”

  • Controller: Generic VVVF drive
  • Where it happens: On start-up after power cycle
  1. Verify mainline and controller fuses are good (no jumpers).
  2. Check safety circuit is closed: stop switch, pit stop, car top, door locks.
  3. Confirm encoder/cable seated and shield grounded.
  4. On the drive: clear faults, check input phases, DC bus OK.
  5. Run an auto-tune/learn per drive manual (no load if required).
  6. Test a slow inspection run ↑/↓; then normal run.

Tip: If the drive faults immediately, check direction phasing and motor leads U/V/W swapped.

Symptom: Doors won’t close, “DLK” not made

  • Controller: Any swing/slider with door operator
  • Where it happens: Lobby floor
  1. Verify door zone sensor and vanes are aligned; clean target.
  2. Inspect interlock circuit continuity from car top to controller.
  3. Check operator limits: fully-close limit and nudging input.
  4. Reduce closing speed/torque for test; check obstructions/rollers.
  5. Simulate DLK with meter only (no jumpers for run); adjust interlock.

Tip: If DLK flickers, suspect broken cable at hinge/loop.
